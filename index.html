Hello! My name is Matthew Pileggi.

Alumnus of University of Florida. Passion for research.

I am a research scientist broadly trained in a variety of fields. My research areas include genomics, bioinformatics, biodiversity and conservation informatics. I started my career in the summer of 2016 at Valencia College in Orlando, Florida my biology professor, Dr. Laurent invited me to participate in a DNA Barcoding research project. After recieving my associate's degree, I then spent the next 4 years at the University of Florida in Gainesville, Florida conducting research while pursuing my bachelor's degree majoring in biology and my master's degree majoring in entomology.

My research so far used digitized specimen descriptions from the New York Botanical Garden to investigate species response to environmental changes, high-throughput sequencing to investigate the prevalence and identities of antibiotic resistant bacteria in flies, and recently an in-house comparative genomic analysis pipeline to identify genomic variations which may account for differences observed in bacterial resistance profiles.
